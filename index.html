"I have a million unfinished Logic projects. Ideas, fragments, sketches that never went anywhere. Not because they weren't good, but because the moment I opened a DAW I was already thinking about production instead of the song."

That was the honest problem. The DAW hijacks the process — you open it and immediately you're thinking about sounds, plugins, and production instead of the actual song.

Drafthaus is the place before the DAW. Think of it as a voice recorder and a notebook in one — with a keyboard, drum machine, sequencer, and looper built in for when you need them. Anywhere, any device, just put the idea down. Work out the chords and lyrics without the pressure of a full session staring back at you.

When something is ready, export it — MIDI, audio sketches, chord charts — and bring it into Logic or Ableton with something real to work from. It's a songwriter's workbench, not a DAW. I hope it's as useful to you as it's been to me.

— Jason, creator of Drafthaus

✍️ Writing

Lyrics, chords, and structure — all in one place.

Each song has its own workspace. Write and edit your lyrics, add chord progressions to any section, and map out the structure of the song — all without leaving the page.

  • Lyrics editor for each song
  • Chord sheets with per-section annotations
  • Structure notes alongside your recordings
  • No switching between apps or windows
✍️ [ Screenshot: lyrics editor with chord annotations ]
🎙 Voice Sketching

Capture the melody before you lose it.

Record a rough vocal, hum the melody, or talk through your ideas. Multi-track recording means you can layer ideas, compare takes, and keep building over time — all inside your song.

  • Multi-track recording, multiple takes per track
  • Visual waveform so you can see what you recorded
  • Recordings are tied to the song and saved automatically
  • Come back any time and pick up where you left off
🎙 [ Screenshot: voice recorder / multi-track waveforms ]
🎹 Chord Sketching

Work out your chord progression visually.

The sequencer isn't for producing — it's for figuring out the chords. Lay out your progression, hear how it sounds, loop it while you sing and write. Connect a MIDI keyboard and just play.

  • Step-sequencer grid with real piano samples
  • Set your tempo, loop your progression
  • Plug in any MIDI keyboard — it just works
  • For sketching ideas, not finishing tracks
🎼 [ Screenshot: sequencer grid with MIDI keyboard ]
🔁 Loop Station

Build up an idea in real time.

The 4-track loop station is for those moments when the idea is flowing. Lay down a strumming pattern, loop it, layer a melody over the top, add harmonies. Keep what's good, save it to the song.

  • 4 independent recording tracks
  • Loops stay locked to your song's BPM
  • Saved loops come back the next time you open the song
  • Great for building up an idea quickly
🔁 [ Screenshot: 4-track loop station ]
👥 Collaborate

Write with someone, wherever they are.

Share any song with a co-writer's Drafthaus account. They hear your sketches, see the lyrics and chords, and can contribute in real time — no shared drives, no file swapping, no "which version is this?"

  • Share individual songs by email address
  • The song appears in their account automatically
  • Real-time sync — everyone sees changes as they happen
  • Available on Drafter and Prolific Drafter plans
